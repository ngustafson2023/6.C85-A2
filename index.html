Overall Analysis Questions

  1. What are the trends in house flipping activity across Boston from 2000 to 2023?
    • Motivation: The increasing trend of house flipping in Boston and its impact on the housing market, as highlighted in "Homes for Profit", motivated this question. This document illustrates the surge in investor activity and its correlation with housing affordability crises. It emphasizes the need to understand flipping trends and their broader implications on housing accessibility.
    • Follow-Up: Which neighborhoods have the highest incidence of house flipping?
    • Follow-Up: How long do properties typically stay in the possession of investors before being flipped?
  2. How has the profitability of house flipping changed over time?
    • Motivation: Insights drawn from "Homes for Profit" about the effects of house flipping on communities, particularly in neighborhoods with vulnerable populations, motivated this question. The report discusses how flipping activities correlate with rising housing prices and affect the availability of affordable housing, prompting a need to explore the profitability shifts of these real estate practices to anticipate community impacts.
    • Follow-Up: How much are properties selling for?
    • Follow-Up: What is the average profit margin on flipped houses, and how has this changed over time?
  3. How significant is the role of institutions in the Boston housing market?
    • Motivation: The article "When Private Equity Becomes Your Landlord" from ProPublica inspired this question. It sheds light on the growing role of large corporations and private equity firms in the housing market, altering the landscape of homeownership and rental markets. This trend raises concerns about the effects of corporate ownership on housing affordability and market dynamics, warranting an in-depth analysis.
    • Follow-Up: What proportion of residential sales is made to institutions versus individuals?
    • Follow-Up: How has corporate buying activity changed over time?

Discoveries & Insights

Summary

In our analysis of residential sales data in the City of Boston from 2000 to 2023, we analyzed three main topics. First, we analyzed how aggregate residential sales changed over time, identifying sharp changes and maximums and minimums, as well as how these sales were distributed amongst different neighborhoods. Second, we analyzed the profitability of house flipping in Boston, and even broke down the data by time horizon. Third, we took a magnifying glass to the dynamics between investor and individual purchases. These visualizations provide ample context to complement the on-going societal debates on how house-flipping and investor behavior might be contributing to the current housing crisis.
